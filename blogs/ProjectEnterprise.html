The Difference Between a Project and an Enterprise (And Why It Matters)

By Enactus MUJ | December 2025

Many students use the terms “project” and “enterprise” interchangeably—but the difference between the two is crucial. A project: Is short-term Ends when funding or deadlines end Often depends on external support An enterprise: Is long-term and continuous Generates its own income Grows and scales over time Most college initiatives are projects. They are designed to finish within a semester or academic year. Enterprises, on the other hand, are built to survive even after students graduate. Enactus encourages students to think beyond grades and certificates. By building enterprises, students learn real-world skills like financial planning, customer validation, operations, and leadership. This distinction matters because real impact comes from continuity. Enterprises don’t stop—they evolve, expand, and multiply their impact year after year.
